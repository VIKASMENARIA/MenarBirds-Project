A Haven for Birds

A Model for the World

Welcome to Menar, a tranquil village just 45 km from Udaipur, which has earned its title as the **'Bird Village' of Rajasthan**. But Menar is more than just a destination; it's a revolutionary story of community-led conservation.

Centered around the **Braham and Dhand lakes**, this isn't a fenced-in park. It's a living wetland complex, passionately protected by its own residents—the **"Pakshi Mitras"** (Friends of Birds). This dedication has earned Menar its designation as a **Ramsar Site**, a Wetland of International Importance.

Why Visit Menar?

An Unforgettable Eco-Journey

A World-Class Avian Paradise

As a Ramsar Site on the Central Asian Flyway, Menar hosts over 250 species of resident and migratory birds. This is a birdwatcher's dream destination.

A Living Legacy of Conservation

Meet the 'Pakshi Mitras'—local villagers who patrol the lakes and protect their winged guests. Your visit directly supports their incredible grassroots efforts.

Rich Cultural Heritage

Menar is steeped in history, home to 600-year-old Nagara-style temples, ancient havelis, and vibrant folk traditions like the 'Gair' dance.

"Here, conservation is not a project; it's a 200-year-old tradition. We invite you to see what happens when a community decides to protect its natural heritage." – A Local 'Pakshi Mitra'
What to See & Do

Immerse Yourself in Menar

Professional Birdwatching

Bring your binoculars! The lakes are a hotspot for spotting Greater Flamingos, Dalmatian Pelicans, and Bar-headed Geese, especially at dawn and dusk.

Village Heritage Walk

Stroll through the lanes to explore ancient temples, intricate havelis, and the 'Historical Top' (cannon point). Discover the warm hospitality of the Mewari people.

Sunrise Photography

The combination of tranquil water, the Aravalli hills, and thousands of birds creates a breathtaking spectacle. The "golden hour" light is magical.

Tours with a 'Pakshi Mitra'

Hire a local guide for the most authentic experience. They can take you to the best viewing spots and share the village's conservation story firsthand.

How to Visit Menar

Best Time to Visit

The **winter season, from October to March**, is the absolute best time. This is when migratory birds arrive in vast numbers, and the weather is pleasant.

How to Reach Menar

Menar is an easy 1-hour drive (45-50 km) from Udaipur. The nearest airport is Maharana Pratap Airport (UDR), Udaipur. You can hire a local taxi for a half-day or full-day trip.

Tips for a Responsible Visit

  • Maintain silence to avoid startling the birds.
  • Do not feed the birds or litter.
  • Hire local guides to support the community.
  • Ask for permission before photographing villagers.
